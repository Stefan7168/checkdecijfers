# Roadmap

Phases 0→3, anchored in the interview decisions ([01-product-vision.md](01-product-vision.md), Decision log). Later-phase candidates mined from the notes are **slotted or explicitly rejected** below — per Stefan's Q1 instruction, nothing gets silently lost. Dates are deliberately absent; phases advance on their gates, not on the calendar.

## Phase 0 — Prove the hard part (current)

Scope and gate live in [03-mvp-scope.md](03-mvp-scope.md). One sentence: question → validated, attributed, deterministic answer (+ simple chart), over 5–10 ingested CBS tables, with refusal behavior — measured by the 20-task benchmark.

**Success:** ≥80% of answerable benchmark tasks fully pass; 100% of refusal tasks pass; zero fabricated numbers; full audit traceability.

## Phase 1 — Private beta with real journalists

**Owner decision, reprioritized (2026-07-04):** billing (credit packs, Stripe/iDEAL, free signup credits — originally Phase 2, below) is pulled forward into this phase, alongside accounts. Reason: the chat is being integrated into a real, publicly-reachable website page imminently — not staying a recruited-only beta — so accounts alone don't close the abuse-protection question ([open-questions #47](open-questions.md)); paid access needs to exist before that page goes live. **Trade-off confirmed (2026-07-04, surfaced explicitly and re-affirmed):** this builds billing infrastructure ahead of the "would you pay per check?" validation the *Success* criteria below were designed to get first — accepted, not a free lunch. Exact scope boundary (e.g. reconciliation, payments-GDPR wording) is decided by the session that builds this, not fixed here. **Separate external dependency, unresolved as of 2026-07-04:** real Stripe payouts need KvK registration + a business bank account first — [RUNBOOK.md](../RUNBOOK.md)'s own checklist calls this "the slowest item," and its status is currently undecided ([open-questions #54](open-questions.md)). Code readiness and payment-readiness are two different clocks — don't assume the second follows automatically from the first.

**Adds:** accounts (identity seam, ADR [006](decisions/006-auth-billing-seams.md)) **and billing** (credit packs via Stripe + iDEAL on the append-only ledger; chat behind account creation with a **one-time free signup credit grant** (100 credits, current value in [09-pricing.md](09-pricing.md)) — [open-questions.md](open-questions.md) #3, resolved 2026-07-05, revised 2026-07-04 — pulled forward from Phase 2 per the decision above); **multi-turn conversation memory** (owner decision 2026-07-05, [open-questions #57](open-questions.md): structured-context merge design, sequenced directly after the access WP — WP15 in [08-build-plan.md](08-build-plan.md); the validation pass measured it as the biggest usability gap); table set grows toward ~25–50 (manual registry + alias workflow — the precursor to demand-driven onboarding, see feature pool; watch the pgvector trigger in ADR [002](decisions/002-postgres-system-of-record.md)); question history (**✅ shipped 2026-07-04, WP17** in [08-build-plan.md](08-build-plan.md): per-user foldable history with per-question net credit cost, on the dashboard alongside balance + top-up); basic **drill-down buttons** on answers, late in the phase (deterministic follow-up queries generated from the answer's own query plan — see feature pool); feedback capture per answer ("klopt dit?"); **claim-verification support** ("klopt het dat X?" gets a verdict via a claim intent type + registered claim-vs-actual derivations — Phase 0 restates the figures without a verdict, see [02-user-scenarios.md](02-user-scenarios.md)); **owner ops-alerting** (batch failure, `needs_review` transition, missed expected sync, health-check failure — the notification seam in [04-architecture.md](04-architecture.md), which magic-link auth's email provider enables anyway); a verified database restore; basic abuse limits (**resolved 2026-07-04:** account required before any question on the main integrated chat, no anonymous trial — [#47](open-questions.md); a separate isolated-budget anonymous-trial experiment is tracked at [#53](open-questions.md), **sequenced as a fast-follow after the paid flow ships (owner decision 2026-07-04) — not built in the same work package**); **free browse layer (start)** — pre-rendered public data pages over already-validated tables, late in the phase (no LLM in the path, SEO compounds slowly — see feature pool); **GDPR checklist for accounts and payments** (activates the reserved section in [04-architecture.md](04-architecture.md) — privacy policy, LLM-provider DPA, retention decisions; **▶ its retention + self-service-deletion piece (#14) is now DO-NOW / front of the plan — owner, session 23; see STATUS.md**); numeric data export (CSV/table) for journalists ([open-questions #52](open-questions.md), resolved into this phase 2026-07-04).

**Success:** ~10–25 active beta journalists (recruited from the freelance/small-newsroom target group); in-the-wild accuracy consistent with the benchmark; qualitative "would you pay per check?" signal — **now gathered concurrently with billing build-out rather than before it** (see the reprioritization note above), so treat early signal here as a course-correction trigger, not just a pre-Phase-2 gate.

## Phase 2 — Paid public launch

**Note (2026-07-04):** billing/credits and account-gated access moved to Phase 1 (see above), pulled forward ahead of the original sequencing. What remains here is the rest of the public-launch surface.

**Adds:** **full browse layer** — complete page inventory plus the chat-teaser signup flow (parsed question shown, validated answer after signup); pre-spend cost transparency (estimate → confirm; a clarification round costs the flat `clarification` price, an outright refusal costs 0 — [09-pricing.md](09-pricing.md); credit-cost labels on drill-down buttons); **shareable answer pages with OpenGraph chart images** (Stefan's programmatic-SEO idea — pages render from audit records; static-image renderer over the chart spec, ADR [007](decisions/007-chart-spec-rendering.md)); watermark/"Gegenereerd via checkdecijfers.nl" on shared visuals; user-facing audit-trail view (the answer's source appendix), **plus a saved/pinned-charts dashboard** — a user marks a past answer's chart as saved, a frozen snapshot (pointer to the existing `audit_answers` row, not a new copy), presentation-only adjustments (reuses the chart-toggle mechanism, [#46](open-questions.md)) — [open-questions #60](open-questions.md), resolved 2026-07-04; **Visualisatie Studio** (social formats, custom sizes, interactivity, embeds — moved forward from Phase 3, Stefan 2026-07-04: it's the same feature surface as the saved-charts dashboard, exporting/customizing a saved chart rather than a separate later build; export ladder unchanged — free watermarked download/embed with backlink → watermark-free huisstijl embed costs credits; one deterministic server-side renderer over the chart spec, ADR [007](decisions/007-chart-spec-rendering.md), never AI); **standalone HTML/JS chart export** (self-contained, offline-capable file — also moved forward from parked/Phase 3, same export-ladder bundle; no backlink/traffic value unlike embeds, kept for AVG/cookie-averse newsrooms and archival use cases); public-launch rate limiting (ADR [005](decisions/005-caching-strategy.md) trigger); daily Stripe-vs-ledger reconciliation with owner alert on mismatch (+ webhook-failure alerting); a support channel with a stated response time; the published-page correction policy implemented ([open-questions.md](open-questions.md) #22).

**Success (indicative, from the notes' Year-1 model — validate, don't worship):** on the order of 250 active users within a year of paid launch; average spend near the notes' ~€12.60/user/mo estimate; repeat-purchase rate demonstrating the no-subscription model retains users.

## Phase 3 — Expansion (options, sequenced by Phase 2 evidence)

**▶ Reprioritized (owner, session 23, 2026-07-05): two items in this list are no longer Phase-3 options.** Demand-driven table onboarding (WP16) is now build **priority #1**, and enrichment / new data sources are **priority #2** — see STATUS.md's TOP PRIORITY STACK + [08-build-plan.md](08-build-plan.md). The rest of this list stays demand-sequenced.

Pick by demand signal, not all at once: **demand-driven table onboarding** (now priority #1 — see above; the "may start late in Phase 2" sequencing below is superseded); **scoop alerts** (seam = ingestion batch diffs, incl. the silent-correction log; the notes priced this at ~€29/mo as a subscription — whether alerts are subscription-priced or credit-funded must be re-decided against the "no subscription" promise, [open-questions.md](open-questions.md) #17); **newsroom licenses** (€250–500/mo indicative; domain access + admin + manual invoicing); **enterprise huisstijl theming**; **premium audit-trail exports** (the notes' "Ironclad Audit Trail" — sell certainty, €300–500/user/mo was the B2B anchor); **enrichment sources** (PDOK/Kadaster, RIVM, UWV, Waarstaatjegemeente, open.overheid.nl as a *document* source type — likely triggers the Python split, ADR [001](decisions/001-single-app-vs-split.md)); **vertical pivot option** (vastgoed/policy B2B per the notes' researched pivot — trigger: journalist TAM proves too small in Phase 2 *and* enrichment sources exist, since that audience buys enriched analysis, not bare CBS lookups); **whitelabel / API-first distribution** (the engine inside partner software — coupled to the vertical pivot, see feature pool).

**Success (option-conditional):** each shipped option must earn its keep within two quarters of launch — alerts: a meaningful share of active users (order of 15%) enables one; newsroom tier: ≥3 paying newsrooms; Studio/exports: measurable share-driven signups; enrichment and pivot options are judged by their trigger conditions, not by ambition.

## Standing revisit triggers (cross-phase)

- Big-tech CBS integration ships with real attribution → re-evaluate moat (vision doc, risk 1).
- CBS re-activates SDMX migration → second `CbsSource` adapter (ADR 003).
- LLM spend > ~€50/mo → prompt caching (ADR 004). Repeated questions > ~20% → answer cache (ADR 005). **Measurement sources** (a trigger nobody measures is decoration): audit records log per-answer latency, token counts, and an intent hash — spend, repeat rate, and p95 derive from them; provider dashboards are checked in the monthly maintenance session ([CLAUDE.md](../CLAUDE.md), conventions).
- Table catalog > ~50 or matching misses → pgvector (ADR 002). Enrichment lands → Python split assessment (ADR 001).
